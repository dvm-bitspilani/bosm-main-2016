How to Reach Pilani?

Pilani can be reached either by rail or by road. The nearest railway stations are Chirawa on Western Railway (16 Km) and Loharu on Northern Railway (24 Km). There are regular bus services between Delhi and Pilani, and Jaipur and Pilani. The buses leave Delhi from the main Inter-State Bus Terminal (ISBT) , Kashmere Gate, and Jaipur from Rajasthan State Roadways Bus stand, Sindhi Camp. From the Delhi airport, direct buses to Chirawa are available from Dhaulakuan/Mahipalpur terminals. Preferably, take only the direct Buses from Delhi/Jaipur to Pilani. Rented taxis are also available from Delhi Jaipur Railway Station/ Bus Stand/ Airport. Please refer to route map to Pilani. The campus is very close to Pilani bus stand.

From Delhi:

The most convenient route to reach Pilani from Delhi is to take a bus from the Inter-State Bus Terminal (ISBT), Kashmere Gate at Delhi, which takes about five and a half hours to reach Pilani. You can also take a train from Sarai Rohilla Railway Station in New Delhi to Loharu Station. The departure timings of trains at Sarai Rohilla Station are 8.50 A.M, 9.15 P.M & 11.00 P.M. Loharu is 25 km away from Pilani. Buses and jeeps are available from Loharu to Pilani. The BITS campus is very close to the Pilani bus stand.

From Jaipur:

You can also board a bus from Rajasthan State Roadways Bus Stand at Sindhi Camp, Jaipur to reach Pilani in around five and a half hours.

This BOSM get Lagori'fied

Young, energetic, full of surprises and utter madness on stage, Lagori has, over the last 4 years, brought thousands to their feet at their concerts. This Bangalore based band comprises 5 unique musicians, one crazier than the other, bringing their own flavours and styles to the table.

Combining Rock, Funk, Pop and Indian Classical inflections Lagori creates a sound that is rich, young, hip, honest and completely infectious! Pronounced ‘La-go-ree’, the band gets its name from the popular street game played in South India.
